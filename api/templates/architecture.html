Step 1

Raw Data

MovieLens ratings and TMDb metadata are merged into a clean movie catalog.

Step 2

CBF Matrix

TF-IDF and cosine similarity are built from genre and language content.

Step 3

Synthetic Cohort

Four known archetypes provide controlled language and genre preferences for testing.

Step 4

Real-only SVD

Collaborative filtering trains only on real MovieLens ratings. No synthetic user rows are used.

Step 5

Evaluation

Synthetic users are split into support history and held-out positive movies for four-model ranking comparison.

What Each Model Proves

CF

Tests standard collaborative filtering trained on real ratings only. It has no access to synthetic archetype patterns.

CBF

Tests whether content similarity from support history can recover held-out liked movies.

NonLocal Hybrid

Tests whether combining CF and CBF helps without demographic language/genre weighting.

Localized Hybrid

Tests the thesis contribution: adaptive CF + CBF + explicit localized preference scoring.

RQ1 Evidence

Precision@10, Recall@10, and NDCG@10 compare the localized hybrid against CF, CBF, and non-local hybrid baselines on the same held-out synthetic positives.

RQ2 Evidence

Filter bubble score, novelty, language diversity, genre diversity, and archetype-level gaps show the ethical tradeoff between cultural alignment and content narrowing.
